The first thing I did was download the historical data for the S&P 500 going back 10 years. Then I tried just a basic prediction 2 years into the future and got this:
It at least looks like stock market-type noise but over the longer term, it is pretty straight compared to the real stock market and is lacking the personality, so I'm not sure how accurate it would be.

After that I wanted to see if it can predict the future in any way at all so I ran it each year back to 2021 and put all the results on a graph.
It clearly has no accuracy predicting the future from this graph, 2024, 2023 and 2022 data all trend the exact same direction over the long term with basically just random noise added on. 2021 was an interesting glitch I will get to next.


For some time periods seemingly at random the prediction would be very straight with minor noise at some points. I tried to figure out what caused it or where exactly it happened but I couldn't figure it out.
I found examples around 2017 and 2021-2022, the one around 2021-22 is cleaner possibly because there is more data beforehand. I think this shows that the AI is not that refined and prone to flaws.


Overall the tech is interesting and cool but not perfect and a horrible predictor of the future market. It definitely has useful functions like the ones they provided in the tutorial sheet but the stock market has far too many variables to be predicted by something like this. It would be interesting to get yearly weekly and daily data and layer them to try to get better prediction or use it for a more specific function like short term day trading with less outside factors.
